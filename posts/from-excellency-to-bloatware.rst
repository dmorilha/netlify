Three decades ago the software industry strove for excellency: a professional would study and be rewarded for good designs in algorithms, complexity, patterns, architectures, abstractions, robustness, etc. Because of greed the industry leaders took a rather different direction in the last twenty years. These principles matter less and consumer's software has become nothing more than bloatware with layers and layers of complexity that can and are exploited for financial gain. Spyware has also revamped as a thing to prevent competition and preserve established monopolies and their hidden deals. It is all a sad state of affairs for those who, after dedicating years and years of study, are at their career midpoint.

Imagine if cars or airplanes were designed like this.
